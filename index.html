Xuying Ning

Ph.D. in Computer Science · University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign

Hi! I am Xuying Ning. I am a PhD student at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, advised by Prof. Jingrui He, and I closely work with Prof. Hanghang Tong.

I am particularly interested in enabling intelligent systems to perform long-horizon multimodal reasoning in real-world environments. Questions that I am excited about include:

  • How to enhance AI agents to solve complex tasks in open-world settings, by augmenting multimodal foundation models with long-horizon search, memory, and tool-use capabilities.
  • How to understand and push the reasoning limits of multimodal foundation models, enabling them to form structured abstractions and relational understanding from unstructured multimodal inputs.
  • How to build self-evolving agents that can adapt, personalize, and explore in open-ended environments, aligning with user needs while improving through interaction.

Prior to this, I received my B.Eng. in Artificial Intelligence from Xi'an Jiaotong University, where I was enrolled in the Honors Youth Program at the age of 15 and the Artificial Intelligence Experimental Class. I also spent time as a visiting student at Columbia University in 2022. I had the privilege of working with Prof. Nanning Zheng and Prof. Qinghua Zheng on research in machine learning and recommender systems.

  • AI Agents: long-horizon reasoning, memory, tool use, and self-evolving behavior in open-world environments
  • Multimodal Reasoning: structured learning for image-text understanding and generation
  • Graph Foundation Models: injecting structural priors into modern multimodal systems
  • Personalization: generative, sequential, and cross-domain recommender systems
